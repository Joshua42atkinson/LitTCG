# LitTCG Brand Guide

## Name

**LitTCG** — spoken "Lit T-T-C"  
**Expansion:** Literary Trading Card Game  
**Never write as:** LitTCG, LITttc, littc, Lit-ttc  
**Always write as:** `LitTCG` (camel case, no spaces)

## Tagline

> *Every word is a card. Every card is a creature.*

Alternate taglines:
- *Spell. Summon. Battle.*
- *The learning is the playing. The playing is the learning.*
- *Where vocabulary becomes a creature collection.*

## Brand Promise

LitTCG is the only game where a real English word becomes a unique collectible creature — and that creature's powers come from what the word actually means.

## Positioning Statement

For homeschooling families with kids ages 6–12, LitTCG is a tactical trading card game that teaches literacy through creature collection and combat. Unlike flashcard apps or math-only learning games, LitTCG makes spelling, synonyms, grammar, and etymology the core game mechanics, not separate educational overlays.

## Voice & Tone

**Voice:** Playful, clever, empowering, transparent  
**Tone:** Game-first, parent-safe, kid-cool

### Do Say
- "Spell a word, summon a pet."
- "Your deck IS your vocabulary."
- "Mastered pets get golden auras and dream poetry."
- "No ads. No subscriptions for kids. One purchase, full curriculum."

### Don't Say
- "Educational software"
- "Vocabulary drill"
- "Digital worksheet"
- "Guaranteed grade improvement"

## Target Audience

### Primary Player
Kids 6–12 who enjoy:
- Pokémon / Yu-Gi-Oh! style collecting
- Slay the Spire / Hearthstone tactics
- Prodigy Math or WonderLang
- Discovering combos and rare finds

### Primary Buyer
Homeschool parents who:
- Want screen time to have educational value
- Avoid subscription traps and microtransactions
- Value offline play and data privacy
- Need visible progress without complex dashboards

## Visual Identity

### Logo Concept
- Wordmark: "LitTCG" in a chunky, slightly magical typeface
- The second "T" doubles as a flipping trading card silhouette
- Optional mascot: Lexi, a friendly Slime pet peeking over the logo

### Color Palette

| Use | Color | Hex |
|-----|-------|-----|
| Primary background | Deep slate | `#1a1a24` |
| UI accent | Bright gold | `#ffd700` |
| Fire element | Ember orange | `#ff6b35` |
| Water element | Aqua blue | `#4ecdc4` |
| Earth element | Clay brown | `#8b5a2b` |
| Air element | Cloud white | `#f7f7f7` |
| Shadow element | Abyss purple | `#6a0dad` |
| Light element | Solar yellow | `#fffacd` |
| Typo/glitch | Magenta | `#ff00ff` |

### Typography
- **Headings:** A bold, rounded display font (e.g., Fredoka One, Baloo 2)
- **Body:** A clean, readable sans-serif (e.g., Nunito, Open Sans)
- **Numbers/stats:** Monospace-adjacent for game-y feel (e.g., Roboto Mono)

### Imagery Style
- 3D procedural pets with element-colored glow
- Trading cards with rarity borders
- Floating holographic UI panels
- Warm, inviting districts with clear grammar color coding

## Lore & World Names

### The Three Classes
- **Slime** — Logic / Dialectic / Semantic analysis
- **Golem** — Grammar / Structure / Syntax
- **Robot** — Rhetoric / Persuasion / Social combat

### RPS Balance
```
Slime beats Robot beats Golem beats Slime
```

### The World
- **12 Districts** with distinct NPCs and themes
- **The Typos** — corrupted word-creatures that players battle
- **The Lexicon** — the realm where all words live as creatures
