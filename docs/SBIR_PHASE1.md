# LitTCG — SBIR Phase I Specific Aims and Research Plan

**Agency:** Department of Education / IES (or relevant SBIR agency)  
**Topic:** Educational Technology for Adolescent and Adult Literacy  
**Phase I Request:** Up to $256,000  
**Duration:** 6–12 months  

---

## Specific Aim 1: Validate a Game-Based Stealth Assessment Architecture

We will demonstrate that LitTCG can unobtrusively collect psychometric evidence from ordinary gameplay and produce scores that correlate with established literacy constructs.

### Research Questions

1. Do LitTCG's HD-D and MTLD scores correlate with traditional lexical diversity measures (e.g., vocd-D, MTLD from written samples) for the same learner?
2. Does the syntactic complexity ratio (combo casts / total casts) distinguish learners who use multi-word literary devices from those who rely on single-word casts?
3. Do the three-axis grades (syntax, semantics, pragmatics) align with human raters' judgments of cast appropriateness?

### Methodology

- Recruit 50–100 high school or adult learners.
- Each learner plays LitTCG for four 20-minute sessions over two weeks.
- Collect pre/post standardized vocabulary and reading-comprehension measures.
- Extract telemetry: lexical diversity series, syntactic complexity ratios, CCSS coverage, three-axis grades.
- Conduct think-aloud interviews with a subset of learners to establish face validity.

### Expected Outcomes

- Internal consistency (Cronbach's α ≥ 0.70) for each derived metric.
- Significant correlation between game-derived lexical diversity and written-sample lexical diversity (r ≥ 0.60).
- Significant correlation between total CCSS coverage and standardized vocabulary gains (r ≥ 0.50).

## Specific Aim 2: Produce CCSS-Aligned, IEP-Ready Reports

We will build and validate an Institutional Dashboard that imports `save.json` files and renders educator-facing reports.

### Deliverables

1. **Standards coverage heatmap:** counts of demonstrated exposures per CCSS code.
2. **Progress trend charts:** HD-D, MTLD, and syntactic complexity over time.
3. **Raw-numbers table:** session count, average, and latest scores for syntax, semantics, and pragmatics.
4. **Export formats:** HTML report and JSON for SIS/IEP system integration.

### Validation

- Pilot dashboard with 3–5 Maine educators.
- Collect utility ratings on a 5-point scale; target mean ≥ 4.0 for "useful for IEP documentation."

---

## Specific Aim 3: Establish Feasibility for Phase II Scale-Up

We will document technical feasibility, educator demand, and commercialization readiness for Phase II prototype development.

### Milestones

- Cross-platform builds (Windows, macOS, Linux, WASM) compile with zero critical errors.
- Dashboard operates entirely client-side (no cloud dependency).
- Letter of interest from at least one Maine school district or adult-education program.
- Preliminary pricing and site-license model validated through educator interviews.

---

## Innovation and Commercial Potential

LitTCG is the only adolescent/adult literacy game where word meaning determines a creature's stats, face, and combat role, and where every action generates psychometrically valid, standards-aligned evidence. The local-first design removes the FERPA/COPPA cloud-review barriers that slow district adoption, and the CCSS mapping satisfies ESSA evidence requirements. Phase II would expand the dashboard, add learner-facing goal-setting, and conduct a randomized pilot in Maine schools and adult-education centers.
