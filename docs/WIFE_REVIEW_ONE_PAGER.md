# LitTCG — One-Page Review for Wife

**What is this?** A game where kids spell real English words to summon unique 3D creatures, then battle and quest with them.

**The hook:** Every word becomes a pet. The pet's color, shape, face, and powers come from the word's actual meaning. "Inferno" looks fiery and angry. "Whisper" looks soft and fast. No two words produce the same creature.

**How it plays (60 seconds):**
1. The child collects floating letters in a 3D world.
2. They arrange the letters into a real word and press Submit.
3. A card flips over and a creature bursts out — the "Pokémon moment."
4. The creature joins the child's collection.
5. The child can use their creatures to battle "Wild Typos" or fill in Mad-Lib quests.

**What the child learns:** Spelling, vocabulary, synonyms, antonyms, grammar, and etymology — without worksheets.

**What makes it special:**
- **Isomorphism:** Spelling is literally casting a spell. Synonyms and antonyms are combat mechanics.
- **Unique IP:** Our FACES protocol turns any English word into a visually distinct creature using its dictionary definition.
- **Ethical design:** No ads, no subscriptions pushed at kids, no cloud tracking.

**Who buys it:**
1. **Homeschool parents** (primary) — 3.4 million kids in the US, $38.6B market.
2. **K–12 schools** (secondary) — Chromebooks are 60% of school devices.

**How we make money:**
- **Free demo:** 10 words, try in a browser.
- **Full game:** $9.99 one-time purchase.
- **Expansion packs:** $4.99 for SAT, science, Spanish, etc.
- **Parent dashboard:** $39.99/year to see what words the child learned and get conversation prompts.

**What we need before selling:**
- A stable web demo that runs on a Chromebook.
- The pet card-flip reveal animation (the emotional hook).
- A parent progress report.
- A simple storefront page.

**Status:** The core engine works. The next 30 days are about polish and the first playable demo.

**Why it matters:** Screen time is unavoidable. Parents want it to be learning time. Kids want it to be fun time. LitTCG is both.
