# 1. Vision and Pedagogy

## The Core Premise: Spelling as Casting
"Communication Class" subverts rote memorization by taking the word "Spelling" literally. In this engine, spelling a word is casting a spell. The child is not learning vocabulary; they are building a biological, magical creature based on the definition and etymology of the word they have assembled.

## Steiner's "Head, Heart, and Hands"
The entire game is mapped to Rudolf Steiner’s holistic Waldorf pedagogy, which emphasizes engaging the child across three vectors:
1. **The Head (Thinking/Semantics)**: The intellectual understanding of a word's meaning. Represented by the **Semantic Slime**, a tank class that absorbs meaning and morphs based on synonyms and antonyms.
2. **The Heart (Feeling/Rhetoric)**: The emotional and pragmatic application of language. Represented by the **Rhetoric Robot**, a support class that uses Kokoro TTS to broadcast logical arguments (Ethos, Pathos, Logos) to manipulate the battlefield.
3. **The Hands (Willing/Syntax)**: The physical action of assembling language. Represented by the **Grammar Golem**, a bruiser class built by physically moving blocks of syntax via ASL gestures in XR space.

## Psycholinguistics
Instead of arbitrary stats, a word's inherent psychological data drives combat:
- **Concreteness** ➔ Attack Power (Logos). Physical objects hit harder than abstract concepts.
- **Valence** ➔ Health (Pathos). Positive words have higher survivability.
- **Dominance** ➔ Defense (Ethos). Words that exert control defend better.
- **Arousal** ➔ Speed. High-energy words attack faster.
