# 3. Summoning and Combat

Combat in Communication Class is not about reducing HP with generic attacks. It is an asymmetrical, tactical puzzle based on semantic relationships.

## The Wild Typos
Enemies in the game are "Typos"—corruptions of language. To defeat them, the player must summon a pet that holds a specific relationship (Synonym or Antonym) to the Typo.

## The Summon Classes
1. **Semantic Slime (Tank)**
   - **Role**: Absorbs massive damage.
   - **Mechanic**: Evolves and grows stronger when fed synonyms. It tanks hits based on the *Concreteness* stat of the word.
2. **Grammar Golem (Bruiser)**
   - **Role**: High physical damage output.
   - **Mechanic**: Its attack power multiplies based on the syntactical complexity of the sentence used to summon it (Subject ➔ Verb ➔ Object).
3. **Rhetoric Robot (Support/Caster)**
   - **Role**: AoE buffs and debuffs.
   - **Mechanic**: Does not deal physical damage. Instead, it uses the "Mouth" (Kokoro TTS) to broadcast logical paradoxes or emotional appeals, manipulating the Typo's attack power or defense.

## The Combat Loop
1. Typo appears and demands a relationship (e.g., "Find an Antonym for HOT").
2. Player physically spells "COLD" using hand tracking.
3. Depending on how the word is cast (gesture intensity) and its classification, the pet spawns and executes its class-specific logic.
